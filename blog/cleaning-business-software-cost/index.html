Cleaning Business Software Cost in 2026: What ZenMaid, Jobber, and Housecall Pro Really Charge

Last week, a cleaning business owner I'd been talking to opened her bookkeeping software, filtered for "software subscriptions" over the past year, and stared at the total. $1,847. She had four cleaners, ten years in business, and she'd been paying her field service software bill every month without ever doing the math.

"I knew it was a lot," she told me. "I didn't know it was that much."

This is the conversation I keep having with cleaning business owners. The monthly invoice feels manageable. The annual total doesn't. And once you start looking at three- and five-year horizons, the number starts to look like a used pickup truck — money you spent renting software you'll never own.

This post breaks down what the three biggest names in cleaning business software actually cost in 2026: ZenMaid, Jobber, and Housecall Pro. All prices below are pulled directly from each company's public pricing page as of May 2026. If you're shopping for the first time, evaluating a switch, or just wondering whether you're overpaying — the numbers should help you think clearly.

A quick note on methodology before we get into it: I'm using the published prices, not the discounted-for-the-first-year-only prices that appear at the top of every SaaS pricing page. Headline promo prices are real for twelve months. The renewal price is real for every month after that, which is the period that actually matters for any business planning to be around in five years. Where a company offers a meaningful annual discount, I've included both numbers so you can see the spread.

ZenMaid pricing in 2026

ZenMaid is the category leader for maid services specifically. It's well-built, well-supported, and the team is responsive. Nothing below is a criticism of the product — it's just the price tag.

ZenMaid uses a base-plus-seat model: a flat monthly fee for the plan, plus a per-cleaner charge on top. From get.zenmaid.com/pricing:

  • Starter — $19/month base + $4/cleaner/month. Capped at 40 appointments per month. SMS charges not included.
  • Pro — $39/month base + $14/cleaner/month. Unlimited appointments. Adds GPS tracking, payroll, QuickBooks sync, digital checklists.
  • Pro Max — $49/month base + $24/cleaner/month. Adds PTO tracking, service ratings, data export, Zapier integration.

Starter looks cheap at $19/month, but the 40-appointment cap means most working businesses outgrow it inside a few months. The plan you'll actually live on is Pro or Pro Max.

For a typical small shop — owner plus five cleaners on Pro — that's $39 + (5 × $14) = $109/month, or $1,308/year. Scale up to twenty cleaners on Pro Max and the bill hits $529/month — $6,348/year.

Two things to remember: SMS charges are billed separately on every tier (if you send automated appointment reminders, that's an add-on), and there's no annual prepay discount — every month is a full-price month.

Published ZenMaid range: $19–$529/month → $228–$6,348/year.

Jobber pricing in 2026

Jobber is broader than ZenMaid — it serves any home-service trade, not just cleaning — and it shows up in the same evaluation set for almost every cleaning business owner I talk to.

Their pricing is more complicated than it looks at first glance because the prices on the homepage are first-year annual-prepaid promo prices. The renewal price is higher. Here's the full picture from getjobber.com/pricing:

Monthly billing, no commitment (the price most owners actually pay long-term):

  • Core — $49/month for 1 user
  • Connect — $139/month for 1 user, or $199/month for the 5-user team plan
  • Grow — $199/month for 1 user, or $399/month for the 10-user team plan
  • Plus — $699/month, includes 15 users

Annual prepaid (headline price, first year only):

  • Core starts at $22/month for 12 months, then renews at $29/month
  • Plus starts at $387/month for 12 months, then renews at $529/month

The honest range to think about is $22–$387/month if you commit annually upfront and stay within the first-year promo — $264–$4,644 in year one. After that, your renewal sits closer to $348–$6,348/year depending on team size.

And the subscription is only part of it. The Receptionist add-on is $99/month, the Marketing Suite is $79/month, and Pipeline is $49/month — all priced on top of whatever plan you're on. Stack two add-ons on the Grow Teams plan and you're at $527/month before card processing fees, which run 2.9% + $0.30 per transaction. On a business doing $25,000/month in card payments, that's another $750/month — separate from the subscription, but very real.

Published Jobber range (first-year annual prepaid): $22–$387/month → $264–$4,644/year.

Housecall Pro pricing in 2026

Housecall Pro is the third name that comes up in nearly every cleaning-business software conversation. They lean heavier on dispatching and field-tech workflows than on cleaning-specific features, but plenty of cleaning operations use them.

From housecallpro.com/pricing as of May 2026:

  • Basic — $59/month annual or $79/month monthly. 1 user. Scheduling, dispatching, quotes, invoices, online booking, review management.
  • Essentials — $149/month annual or $189/month monthly. Up to 5 users. Adds QuickBooks sync, GPS tracking, marketing tools, equipment tracking.
  • MAX — $299/month annual or $329/month monthly. Up to 8 users. Adds advanced reporting, dedicated onboarding, escalated phone support. Additional users cost $35/month each.

On annual billing, the published range is $59–$299/month, or $708–$3,588/year. On monthly billing it's $79–$329/month. That's before any extra users on MAX (which most growing teams need) and before payment processing.

For a 10-cleaner team on MAX, you're at $299 + (2 extra users × $35) = $369/month for the subscription alone — $4,428/year. And that's before the Sales Proposal Tool, the HCP Assist call-answering service, or the Voice features Housecall Pro prices separately. Card processing on their platform starts at 2.59%, slightly better than the other two, but still a real line item at any volume.

Published Housecall Pro range (annual): $59–$299/month → $708–$3,588/year.

The 3-year compounding cost

Monthly software fees feel like background noise. Three-year totals don't.

Here's what a cleaning business owner with five cleaners actually pays across three years, assuming a realistic mid-tier plan on each platform and no upgrades, add-ons, or growth:

Software Monthly Year 1 Year 2 Year 3 3-Year Total
ZenMaid Pro (owner + 5 cleaners) $109 $1,308 $1,308 $1,308 $3,924
Housecall Pro Essentials (5 users, annual) $149 $1,788 $1,788 $1,788 $5,364
Jobber Connect Teams (5 users) $199 $2,388 $2,388 $2,388 $7,164

Those numbers assume you never upgrade tiers, never add a sixth cleaner, never opt into a single add-on, and never pay a payment processing fee. In real life, almost every business does at least one of those things — usually all of them — and the three-year total climbs higher.

A used Honda Civic with 100,000 miles runs about $7,500 in this market. That's roughly what a five-cleaner shop pays Jobber over three years.

The difference: at the end of three years, you own the car.

The numbers also assume zero growth. If you add a sixth cleaner in year two and a seventh in year three — a normal trajectory for a healthy small cleaning business — your Jobber Connect Teams plan crosses into Grow Teams territory ($349–$399/month) and the three-year total starts pushing past $8,500. ZenMaid scales smoother because of the per-seat model, but each cleaner you hire adds another $14–$24/month in perpetuity. Growth is supposed to make a business stronger. With SaaS, it also makes your software vendor stronger.

Now stretch the view to five years. Five years of Jobber Connect Teams at $199/month is $11,940. Five years of Housecall Pro Essentials at $149/month is $8,940. Five years of ZenMaid Pro for an owner plus five cleaners is $6,540. None of those numbers include the add-ons, the upgrades, the new cleaner you'll hire, or the annual price increases that every SaaS vendor takes once or twice a decade.

What cleaning business owners actually need

Here's the part of the conversation that's always the most interesting. When I ask a cleaning business owner what they actually use their software for, the answer is almost always the same short list:

  • Putting jobs on a calendar and dispatching cleaners to them
  • Sending appointment reminders to clients automatically
  • Sending invoices and getting paid
  • Keeping client notes — gate codes, pet warnings, "don't use bleach in the master bath"
  • Letting cleaners see their schedule from their phones

That's the daily reality. Five things.

What they're paying $109–$199/month for is a platform that also includes AI receptionists, marketing campaign tools, lead pipelines, custom automation builders, vehicle GPS dashcams, business coaching workshops, integrated payroll, sales proposal builders, and a few dozen other features they will never open.

There's nothing wrong with bundled platforms — some businesses use those extras and get genuine value out of them. But if you're a typical 2–10 cleaner shop and the list above describes how you actually use your software, you're paying for the features the price tag was designed around, not the features you actually need.

The question worth doing the math on for your specific business: if you could get the five things you actually use — plus reliable updates and real support — for a one-time payment in the range of one to two years of your current subscription, would that be a better deal over five years?

That's the math this whole industry is starting to wrestle with. The subscription model made sense when cloud infrastructure was expensive and self-hosting was a developer's full-time job. Both of those things have changed. The case for paying $109/month forever to a vendor who pockets the difference is getting harder to defend.
