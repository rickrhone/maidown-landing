Cleaning Business Software Cost in 2026: What ZenMaid, Jobber, and Housecall Pro Really Charge

$3,924 for ZenMaid Pro. $5,364 for Housecall Pro Essentials. $7,164 for Jobber Connect Teams.

That's what a single small cleaning business with five cleaners pays each of these platforms over three years — no upgrades, no add-ons, no growth, no payment processing fees.

And at the end of those three years, the owner doesn't own any of it. Stop paying, and the software stops working.

When we started looking seriously at the cleaning business software market, the numbers stopped us. Most cleaning business owners have never sat down to total it up. The monthly invoices are small enough to absorb without thinking. The annual totals aren't. The three-year totals really aren't.

In this post we break down what the three biggest names in cleaning business software actually cost in 2026: ZenMaid, Jobber, and Housecall Pro. All prices below are pulled directly from each company's public pricing page as of May 2026. If you're shopping for the first time, evaluating a switch, or just wondering whether you're overpaying — the numbers should help you think clearly.

A quick note on methodology before we get into it: we focus on the standard published prices, not the discounted first-year prices that appear at the top of most pricing pages. Those introductory discounts are real for twelve months, but the renewal price is what you actually pay over the long haul. Where a company offers a meaningful annual discount, we've shown both numbers so you can see the difference.

ZenMaid pricing in 2026

ZenMaid is the category leader for maid services. It's well-built, well-supported, and the team is responsive. Nothing below is a criticism of the product — it's just the price tag.

ZenMaid charges in two parts: a flat monthly fee for the plan, plus an additional charge for each cleaner you add. From get.zenmaid.com/pricing:

  • Starter — $19/month base + $4/cleaner/month. Capped at 40 appointments per month. Text message charges not included.
  • Pro — $39/month base + $14/cleaner/month. Unlimited appointments. Adds GPS tracking, payroll, QuickBooks sync, digital checklists.
  • Pro Max — $49/month base + $24/cleaner/month. Adds PTO tracking, service ratings, data export, Zapier integration.

Starter looks cheap at $19/month, but the 40-appointment cap means most working businesses outgrow it inside a few months. The plan you'll actually live on is Pro or Pro Max.

For a typical small shop — owner plus five cleaners on Pro — that's $39 + (5 × $14) = $109/month, or $1,308/year. Scale up to twenty cleaners on Pro Max and the bill hits $529/month — $6,348/year.

Two things to remember: text message charges (used to send appointment reminders to your clients) cost extra on every tier, and there's no annual discount — every month is a full-price month.

Published ZenMaid range: $19–$529/month → $228–$6,348/year.

Jobber pricing in 2026

Jobber is broader than ZenMaid — it serves any home-service trade, not just cleaning — and it shows up in the same shortlist for almost every cleaning business owner shopping for software.

Jobber's pricing is more complicated than it looks at first because the headline numbers on their homepage are first-year discount prices. The renewal price is higher. Here's the full picture from getjobber.com/pricing:

Paid month-to-month, no commitment (the price most owners actually pay over the long haul):

  • Core — $49/month for 1 user
  • Connect — $139/month for 1 user, or $199/month for the 5-user team plan
  • Grow — $199/month for 1 user, or $399/month for the 10-user team plan
  • Plus — $699/month, includes 15 users

Paid for the full year upfront (the discounted price, first year only):

  • Core starts at $22/month for 12 months, then renews at $29/month
  • Plus starts at $387/month for 12 months, then renews at $529/month

The honest range to think about is $22–$387/month if you pay for a full year upfront and stay within the first-year discount window — $264–$4,644 in year one. After that, your renewal sits closer to $348–$6,348/year depending on team size.

And the subscription is only part of it. The Receptionist add-on is $99/month, the Marketing Suite is $79/month, and Pipeline is $49/month — all priced on top of whatever plan you're on. Add two of these to the Grow Teams plan and you're at $527/month before credit card processing fees, which run 2.9% + $0.30 per transaction. On a business doing $25,000/month in card payments, that's another $750/month — separate from the subscription, but very real.

Published Jobber range (first-year discount): $22–$387/month → $264–$4,644/year.

Housecall Pro pricing in 2026

Housecall Pro is the third name that consistently comes up when cleaning business owners compare software. They lean heavier on dispatching and field-tech workflows than on cleaning-specific features, but plenty of cleaning operations use them.

From housecallpro.com/pricing as of May 2026:

  • Basic — $59/month when paid annually, or $79/month when paid month-to-month. 1 user. Scheduling, dispatching, quotes, invoices, online booking, review management.
  • Essentials — $149/month annual or $189/month monthly. Up to 5 users. Adds QuickBooks sync, GPS tracking, marketing tools, equipment tracking.
  • MAX — $299/month annual or $329/month monthly. Up to 8 users. Adds advanced reporting, dedicated onboarding, escalated phone support. Additional users cost $35/month each.

When paid annually, the published range is $59–$299/month, or $708–$3,588/year. Paid month-to-month it's $79–$329/month. That's before any extra users on MAX (which most growing teams need) and before credit card processing.

For a 10-cleaner team on MAX, you're at $299 + (2 extra users × $35) = $369/month for the subscription alone — $4,428/year. And that's before the Sales Proposal Tool, the HCP Assist call-answering service, or the Voice features, all priced separately. Card processing on their platform starts at 2.59%, slightly better than the other two, but still a real line item at any volume.

Published Housecall Pro range (annual): $59–$299/month → $708–$3,588/year.

The 3-year compounding cost

Pay $149 a month and it barely registers. Pull up your bank statement three years later, total the line items under one software vendor's name, and the number can stop you cold.

Here's what a cleaning business owner with five cleaners actually pays across three years, assuming a realistic mid-tier plan on each platform and no upgrades, add-ons, or growth:

Software Monthly Year 1 Year 2 Year 3 3-Year Total
ZenMaid Pro (owner + 5 cleaners) $109 $1,308 $1,308 $1,308 $3,924
Housecall Pro Essentials (5 users, annual) $149 $1,788 $1,788 $1,788 $5,364
Jobber Connect Teams (5 users) $199 $2,388 $2,388 $2,388 $7,164

Those numbers assume you never upgrade your plan, never add a sixth cleaner, never opt into a single add-on, and never pay a credit card processing fee. In real life, almost every business does at least one of those things — usually all of them — and the three-year total climbs higher.

A used Honda Civic with 100,000 miles runs about $7,500 in this market. That's roughly what a five-cleaner shop pays Jobber over three years.

The difference: at the end of three years, you own the car.

The numbers also assume zero growth. If you add a sixth cleaner in year two and a seventh in year three — which is what growth looks like for a healthy small cleaning business — your Jobber Connect Teams plan crosses into Grow Teams territory ($349–$399/month) and the three-year total climbs past $8,500. ZenMaid scales more smoothly because each cleaner adds a fixed amount to the bill — but each cleaner you hire adds another $14–$24/month, and that charge never stops. Growth is supposed to make a business stronger. With monthly software, it also makes your software vendor stronger.

Now stretch the view to five years. Five years of Jobber Connect Teams at $199/month is $11,940. Five years of Housecall Pro Essentials at $149/month is $8,940. Five years of ZenMaid Pro for an owner plus five cleaners is $6,540. None of those numbers include the add-ons, the upgrades, the new cleaner you'll hire, or the yearly price increases that most software companies push through once or twice a decade.

What cleaning business owners actually need

Browse cleaning business Facebook groups, Reddit threads, and software reviews on sites like Capterra and G2, and the same short list of features comes up over and over when owners describe what they actually use their software for:

  • Putting jobs on a calendar and assigning cleaners to them
  • Sending appointment reminders to clients automatically
  • Sending invoices and getting paid
  • Keeping client notes — gate codes, pet warnings, "don't use bleach in the master bath"
  • Letting cleaners see their schedule from their phones

That's the daily reality. Five things.

What owners are paying $109–$199/month for is a platform that also includes AI receptionists, marketing campaign tools, lead pipelines, custom automation builders, vehicle GPS dashcams, business coaching workshops, integrated payroll, sales proposal builders, and a few dozen other features they will never open.

There's nothing wrong with all-in-one software — some businesses use those extras and get real value out of them. But if you run a typical 2–10 cleaner shop and the list above describes how you actually use your software, you're paying for the features the price tag was designed around, not the features you actually need.

The question worth doing the math on for your specific business: if you could get the five things you actually use — plus reliable updates and real support — for a one-time payment in the range of one to two years of your current subscription, would that be a better deal over five years?

That's the math the whole industry is wrestling with. The monthly-subscription model made sense back when running software took real technical skill and the cost of hosting it was high. Neither of those things is true anymore. The case for paying $109/month forever to a software company that pockets the difference grows weaker every year.
